General information
This privacy policy (the "Privacy Policy") describes the personal data we collect about you. It also details how we use, disclose and protect this data, as well as your access and correction rights over this data.
By continuing to use these platforms, you signify that you have read, understood and agree to be bound by this Privacy Policy as amended from time to time in respect of our  collection, use and disclosure of your personal data. 
 
Collection of your personal data
We collect personal data about you from a variety of sources, including data which we collect from you directly.
When you access our platforms, we may also collect further information from your usage of the platforms, including your frequency of use, pages viewed, geo-location data, originating domain name of a user’s internet service provider, Cookies, IP addresses and type of browser, which we will use for administration purposes and statistical analysis. 
 
How we use your personal data
We use your personal data to provide, as well as to improve our digital services, websites, various mobile applications and consumer programmes. 
We may use your personal data for one or more of the following purposes:
• Technical
To enable the efficient working of our websites 
• Ease of use
To make navigation on our digital platforms (e.g. website, mobile applications) easier for you, including but not limited to:
a) Remembering your language, country selections, previous interactions/purchases and presenting relevant information;
b) Keeping track of your membership details when you have logged in
• Tracking
To gather statistics on how you navigate and transact on our websites, including but not limited to:
a) Gathering feedback on your satisfaction through a survey; and
b) Gaining insight into how you navigate and transact on our websites so that improvements can be made to its usability.
• Marketing
To enable us to deliver a more customized and enjoyable user experience to you, including but not limited to:
a) Allowing our websites to serve up different versions of a page for marketing purposes;
b) Allowing third parties to display appropriate advertising and to track its effectiveness; and
c) Displaying of relevant messages, which offer a selection of products based on your viewing behaviour on our websites and other selected websites. 
 
Other purposes of data collection and use include:
• To help us identify you and any digital accounts you hold with us
• Administration 
• Customer service (e.g. responding to queries, requests, feedback and complaints)
• Statistical research and analytics (including statistical analysis)
• Customer profiling and analysing your purchasing preferences
• Fraud prevention and detection
• Billing and order fulfillment
• To notify you of any changes to our platforms or services which may affect you (e.g. programme policies, terms and conditions, platform updates and other administrative information)
• Security vetting
• To administer contest, sweepstakes or other similar promotions, including announcing the results of lucky draws promotions, contests, identifying and contacting the winners
• Such other purpose as may be specified in a data protection notice given to you at the time your data is collected
We may use the data you have provided us in any of the services and promotions offered by CAG. We will collect, combine and integrate this data collected from you across CAG, in order to offer you more tailored content and services. 
We may share your personal data with our different departments and with third parties such as fulfillment houses and advertising agencies who assist us with our promotional, marketing and research activities. We impose obligations on any external organizations with which we share your personal data to maintain the integrity of that information.

Use of Cookies
We may also use "Cookies" – which are small files stored on a user's computer. By using our platforms, you consent to our use of Cookies to collect and use your personal data in order to collect website usage data, and improve your online experience. The Cookies hold a small amount of information specific to you, and remember your specific activities on the Portal.
By doing so, they enable us to customise your future visits. We do not use Cookies to collect any individually-identifiable information.
Your computer can be programmed to inform you each time a Cookie is being used in this manner. If you wish, your computer can also be configured to prevent the use of Cookies. If you choose to disable all Cookies we cannot guarantee the performance of our platforms and some features may not work as expected.
 
Partnership with Adara and Sojern 
We partner advertising networks - Adara (Adara.com) and Sojern (Sojern.com) - to capture non-personally identifiable data from you through third-party Cookies. This enables Adara and Sojern to display targeted and relevant messages from selected third-party websites, based on your viewing behavior on the our websites and other selected websites.
Your non-personally identifiable data is shared by us with Adara and Sojern in the strictest confidence, for the unique purpose of serving more relevant advertising. Standards have been defined to ensure appropriate advertising is served, and all served advertisements offer the possibility for you to opt out of such selective advertising by Adara and Sojern. However, we have no control over the use of Cookies by Adara and Sojern.
There are two ways for you to opt-out of Cookie use:
For Adara
• By visiting http://adara.com/privacy-promise/opt-out and clicking ‘Submit’; or 
• Through the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) or the Digital Advertising Alliance’s (DAA) Self-Regulatory Program for Online Behavioral Advertising, of which Adara is member. Each of these programmes provides an opt-out page where you can exercise control over members/’participants’ Cookies from a central location.
For Sojern
• By visiting http://preferences-mgr.truste.com/?affiliateId=111, checking the opt-out box and clicking ‘Submit Opt-outs’; or 
• Through the Digital Advertising Alliance’s (DAA) Self-Regulatory Program for Online Behavioral Advertising, of which Sojern is a member. This programme provides an opt-out page where you can exercise control over members/’participants’ Cookies from a central location.
• To opt-out of NAI members’ or DAA participants’ Cookies, visit:
o NAI: http://www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp
o DAA: http://www.aboutads.info/choices/

Keeping your data secure
We will use technical and organisational measures to safeguard your personal data, for example:
• Access to your account is controlled by a password and username which are unique to you
• We store your personal data on secured servers in hashed data format
• Payment details are encrypted
• Your personal data will be retained in a secure environment and access to it will be restricted according to the ‘need to know' principle.
 
Whilst we will use all reasonable efforts to safeguard your personal data, you acknowledge that the use of our platforms is not entirely secure and for this reason we cannot guarantee the security or integrity of any personal data which are transferred from you or to you via these platforms.
We will retain your personal data for as long as it is necessary to fulfill the legal or business purpose for which it was collected. 
When destroying your personal data, we will take commercially reasonable and technically possible measures to make the personal information irrecoverable or irreproducible in accordance with the applicable laws.
Upon submission of a request to destroy or remove your personal data from our records, please note that your request will be processed within a period of 5 to 7 working days. (Updated as of 11 July 2017)
