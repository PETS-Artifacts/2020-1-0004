What information do we collect
When you visit oceanfm.ie, the website sets cookies on your computers. Cookies can store information about you and your device, including your IP address, geographical location, browser, operating system, how you interact with our website, and other information about your web browsing activity. This information is collected by cookies set by Google Analytics and advertising services like DoubleClick and Quantcast. We use Google Analytics to measure statistics about the website, like page views and audience demographic. We use advertising services to display ads across the website, and those services collect information about users in order to optimise and personalise the ads. Other cookies are set by media players from YouTube and SoundCloud. We embed their media players on the website. And lastly, we use cookies to monitor website security.
You can choose not to accept cookies. If you do not accept cookies you can continue to use the website normally.
For details, see our Cookie Policy.
Security
The oceanfm.ie website has security measures in place to protect any information collected on it. Your connection to the website is a secure HTTPS connection. However, we cannot guarantee the security of any information transmitted over the internet. We make every effort to protect the information that we collect and process.
Right of access, rectification or erasure
Any individual has a right to make an access request, in writing, under Section 4 of the Data Protection Acts, for a copy of their personal data held by Ocean FM. To make an access request, please email us at emma.burke@oceanfm.ie. We will comply within 30 days of the receipt of such a request. 
Any individual also has the right, under section 6 of the Data Protection Acts, to have their personal data corrected or erased. To request a correction or to erase any data we may hold, please email emma.burke@oceanfm.ie. We will comply within 30 days of the receipt of such a request.
Any individual has the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority (Data Protection Commission) if they are not happy with any aspect of the way we have collected or processed their personal data.
Cookie policy
What is a cookie
A cookie is a small file stored on your computer or mobile device. Websites use cookies to store and retrieve information about users, which enables features that need to to “remember” and track users. For example, cookies allow websites to remember what items are in your shopping cart, or whether you are logged in or logged out.
There are two types of cookie: session and persistent. A session cookie is deleted from your device when you leave the website. A persistent cookie has an expiry time, which can range from hours to years. A persistent cookie will remain on your computer until it is manually deleted or it expires.
Cookies can come from two sources: first-party or third-party. A first-party cookie is a cookie set by the website you are visiting. A third-party cookie is included on the website you are visiting, but comes from another domain.
How are cookies used on this website
This website uses both session and persistent cookies, and both first-party and third-party cookies.
