Privacy Policy of IslamicFinder.org

Protecting your privacy is very important to us. We’re telling you about our privacy policy so you know what information we collect, why we collect it, and what we do with it. This privacy notice is for IslamicFinder.org. This site is referred to as “IslamicFinder.org” or “IF” throughout the rest of this notice and is maintained and operated by IslamicFinder. Operating IslamicFinder.org, does not collect your name, email address, location or other similar information unless you choose to provide it. We do collect other limited information automatically from visitors who read, browse, and/or download information from our site. We do this so we can understand how the site is being used and how we can make it more helpful. If you create a user account by signing up from IslamicFinder, and use our module on the site (such as PrayerBook), we save your data of prayer logging that you added from our Athan application. We keep your data save of complete prayer logging throughout all past months and years. This information is collected in order to give you full information about your prayers progress in statistical form i.e. offered prayers, missed prayers, most offered prayers, most missed prayer and best day throughout the month. The important point is that IslamicFinder keeps users information confidential and does not sell any information that we got after your sign up process to any third party.

Types of information we collect

Information which is automatically collected:

When you browse

Certain information about your visit can be collected when you browse our website. When you browse IslamicFinder.org, we, and in some cases our third-party service providers, can collect the following types of information about your visit, including:

  • IP address (an IP or internet protocol address is a number that is automatically assigned to a device connected to the web)
  • Approximate geographic location based on the IP address of the user’s local system or internet service provider.
  • Operating system (which is software that directs a computer’s basic functions such as executing programs and managing storage) for the device that you are using and information about the browser you used when visiting the site.
  • Date and time of your visit.
  • Pages you visited.
  • Address of the website that connected you to IslamicFinder.org (such as google.com)
  • Device type (desktop computer, tablet, or type of mobile device)
  • Screen resolution
  • Browser language
  • Geographic location
  • Time spent on pages
  • Scroll depth – The measure of how much of a web page was viewed
  • User events (e.g. clicking a button)
  • Cookies of prayer times setting i.e. Calculation method, juristic method, Latitude and Longitude of your selected location.

We use this information to measure the number of visitors to our site and its various sections, to help make our site more useful to visitors, and to improve our public education and outreach through digital advertising. Also, this information is sometimes used to personalize the content we show to you on third-party sites.

These third-party services may collect information through the use of web beacons that are located on our pages. A web beacon is a see-through graphic image (usually 1 pixel x 1 pixel) that is placed on a web page and, in combination with a cookie, allows us to collect information regarding the use of the web page that contains the web beacon. We use web beacons to tell when a user is redirected to IslamicFinder.org because of a Locations advertisement that we ran on another website was clicked on or otherwise interacted with (known as “click tracking” or “conversion tracking”) and to better target Locations advertisements (known as “retargeting”). uses third-party tools to help deliver advertising. Improve your location’s accuracy

Sometimes we might have trouble finding where you are located. Having your current location will help us to get you more accurate prayer times and nearby Islamic places. Here are some things you can do to help fix the problem.

  • Chrome
  • Safari

  • iOS

  2. Click Settings and then Show advanced settings.
  3. In the "Privacy" section, click Content settings.
    1. In the dialog that appears, scroll down to the "Location" section. Select one of these permissions:
    2. Allow all sites to track your physical location: Select this option to let all sites automatically see your location.
    3. Ask when a site tries to track your physical location: Select this option if you want Google Chrome to alert you whenever a site wants to see your location.
    4. Do not allow any site to track your physical location: Select this option if don't want any sites to see your location.
  4. Click Done.

  2. To allow for changes, click the lock in the bottom left.
  3. Check "Enable Location Services."

    1. On your phone or tablet, open the Settings app.
    2. Tap Location.
    3. At the top, switch location on.
    4. Tap Mode and then High accuracy.

  2. Open Chrome
    1. In the top right, tap More
    2. Tap Settings.
    3. Under "Advanced", tap Site Settings

      2. If you see "blocked" under "Location," tap Blocked > tap IslamicFinder > Clear & reset.
