Privacy Policies

What is this Privacy Policy for?

This privacy policy is for IslamicFinder's website [www.islamicfinder.org] and served by IslamicFinder and maintains the privacy of its users.

The policy addresses the various areas where user privacy is concerned and defines the obligations and requirements of the website users, the website itself and the website owners. Moreover, the way this website protects, stores and processes user data and information will also be given in detail in this policy.

The Website

The IslamicFinder's website and it's owners take a proactive approach regarding user privacy and make sure that the necessary steps are taken to protect the privacy of its users throughout their website browsing experience.

Athan by IslamicFinder

Athan is a flagship app of IslamicFinder. We at Athan value our user's privacy and collect their information only after their consent so that they can make the most of our apps features and have an overall better experience. Wherever we collect sensitive information (such as location), its encrypted and transmitted to us in a secure way. We need permission to access user location, media, camera, call information and address book. We also need permissions to send notifications and for in-app purchases.

Location information is required for providing our users accurate Prayer Times, Qibla direction, information about Places around them and Islamic Calendar for date. Notification permission is required for sending prayer times and other helpful reminder notifications. Access to user's media storage is required for downloading translations of Quran and for sharing Prayer times and Duas. User's camera permission is required for updating their profile picture and in-app purchases permission is needed to allow the user to buy our exciting promotions. Access to their call information and address book is required to help them contact any Islamic organization and invite their friends and family to use our app respectively.

Please note that Athan would be able to access only what the user wants to share. None of this information is tracked or shared with third parties and other companies. Keeping our user's personal information safe is our priority.

Use of Cookies

The IslamicFinder website uses cookies to improve the users experience while browsing/visiting the website. This website uses a cookie control system which enables the user to allow or disallow the use of cookies on their computer / device upon their first website visit. This complies with recent legislation requirements for websites to attain explicit consent from their users before leaving behind or reading files such as cookies on a user's computer / device.

Cookies are small files that are saved to the user's computer's hard drive which track, save and store information about the user's interactions and usage of the website. This allows the website to provide its users with a personalized experience.

In order for our users to disallow or discontinue the use and saving of cookies from this website on their computer's hard drive, necessary steps will have to be taken within their web browsers security settings which will block all website cookies and it's external serving vendors.

This website uses tracking software to monitor it's visitors to better understand their website usage. This tracking software is provided by Google Analytics, which uses cookies to monitor visitor's usage. The software will automatically save a cookie to your computer's hard drive in order to track your engagement and usage of the IslamicFinder website, but it won't collect, save or store personal information of users. You can read Google's privacy policy for further information [http://www.google.com/privacy.html].

Other cookies might be saved to your computer's hard drive by external websites or vendors when this website uses referral programs, sponsored links or advertisements. Such cookies are used for conversion and referral tracking, which typically expire within 30 days, though some may take longer. No personal information is saved, collected or stored.

Sometimes we might have trouble finding where you are located. Having your current location will help us to get you more accurate prayer times and nearby Islamic places. Here are some things you can do to help fix the problem.

  • Chrome
  • Safari
  • Android
  • iOS
  1. In the top right, click More
  2. Click Settings and then Show advanced settings.
  3. In the "Privacy" section, click Content settings.
    1. In the dialog that appears, scroll down to the "Location" section. Select one of these permissions:
    2. Allow all sites to track your physical location: Select this option to let all sites automatically see your location.
    3. Ask when a site tries to track your physical location: Select this option if you want Google Chrome to alert you whenever a site wants to see your location.
    4. Do not allow any site to track your physical location: Select this option if don't want any sites to see your location.
  4. Click Done.

  3. Check "Enable Location Services."

    2. Tap Location.
    3. At the top, switch location on.
    4. Tap Mode and then High accuracy.

  2. Open Chrome
    1. In the top right, tap More
    2. Tap Settings.
    3. Under "Advanced", tap Site Settings

      2. If you see "blocked" under "Location," tap Blocked > tap IslamicFinder > Clear & reset.
